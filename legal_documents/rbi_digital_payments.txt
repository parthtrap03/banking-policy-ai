# RBI DIGITAL PAYMENT GUIDELINES AND UPI REGULATIONS
# Reserve Bank of India
# National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI)
# Consolidated Reference Document

## CHAPTER 1: UNIFIED PAYMENTS INTERFACE (UPI) FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Overview
The Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is a real-time payment system developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) under the regulatory oversight of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).
UPI facilitates inter-bank peer-to-peer and person-to-merchant transactions through mobile devices.
As of 2025, UPI operates in 25 countries including Singapore, Bhutan, Malaysia, UAE, France, Oman, Qatar, Mauritius, and Israel.

### 1.2 Transaction Limits

Standard UPI Transaction Limits:
- General person-to-person (P2P) transactions: Rs. 1,00,000 per transaction
- Hospitals and educational institutions: Rs. 5,00,000 per transaction (increased from Rs. 1,00,000 in December 2023)
- Tax payments via UPI: Rs. 5,00,000 per transaction (increased from Rs. 1,00,000 in August 2024)
- IPO applications: Rs. 5,00,000 per transaction (from December 2021)
- Capital markets, insurance, and mutual funds: Enhanced limits applicable

UPI Lite (On-Device Wallet) Limits:
- Per-transaction maximum: Rs. 500 (proposed increase to Rs. 1,000)
- Wallet balance limit: Rs. 2,000 (proposed increase to Rs. 5,000)
- Daily spending cap: Rs. 4,000
- Auto top-up: Maximum 5 times daily

UPI 123PAY (Feature Phone) Limits:
- Transaction limit: Rs. 10,000 (increased from Rs. 5,000 in October 2024)

UPI Circle (Delegated Payments) Limits:
- Monthly limit per secondary user: Approximately Rs. 15,000
- Per-transaction limit: Approximately Rs. 5,000
- Maximum 5 secondary users per primary user

### 1.3 KYC and Authentication Requirements

User Verification:
- KYC-linked mobile number is mandatory for creation of Virtual Payment Address (VPA)
- Multi-factor authentication required for transactions exceeding Rs. 1,00,000 for credit card repayments, mutual funds, and insurance premiums
- Four-digit or six-digit UPI PIN is standard for transaction authentication
- Two-factor authentication flow mandatory for UPI 123PAY peer-to-peer transactions

Biometric Authentication:
- Biometric identification proposed using fingerprints on Android and Face ID on iPhones
- Secondary users require app passcodes and biometric verification

UPI One World (International Visitors):
- Passport and valid visa required for KYC verification
- Available through approved Prepaid Payment Instrument (PPI) issuers at airports, hotels, and money exchange sites
- Foreign exchange restrictions apply to unused balances (returnable to original payment source)

### 1.4 Merchant Guidelines

Market Share Restrictions:
- Third-party payment providers limited to 30% market share calculated on a rolling 3-month basis
- Calculation baseline: 1 January 2021
- Compliance deadline extended to December 2024 for major providers
- Non-compliance triggers penalties under UPI procedural guidelines

Merchant Discount Rate (MDR):
- MDR was nullified in 2019 by the Ministry of Finance to encourage adoption of digital payments for low-value transactions
- RBI is considering reintroduction of MDR for economic sustainability of payment service providers

Bank Participation:
- 304 banks linked to UPI platform as of February 2022
- Payment Service Providers (PSPs) operate their own mobile applications
- Issuers may rely on third-party software for transaction processing

## CHAPTER 2: COMPLAINT AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 2.1 Transaction Failure Resolution
- Real-time feature reducing fund release time from 24 hours to 30 seconds for transaction declines (launched September 2022)
- Online dispute resolution capabilities mandatory across all UPI transactions
- RBI mandates compensation for unsuccessful transactions through authorized payment methods

### 2.2 Grievance Redressal Framework
- Level 1: Payment Service Provider (PSP) app-level grievance mechanism
- Level 2: Bank-level nodal officer
- Level 3: RBI Ombudsman for Digital Transactions
- Complaints must be resolved within 30 days

### 2.3 Transaction Visibility
- All secondary user transactions must appear on primary user's bank statement and UPI app
- Transaction history must be maintained for minimum 10 years

## CHAPTER 3: SECURITY REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Authentication Mechanisms
- Four-digit or six-digit UPI PIN standard for all transactions
- Dual Tone Multi-Frequency (DTMF) signaling for voice-based payments via UPI 123PAY
- Multi-factor authentication activated for mandates exceeding Rs. 1,00,000
- Device binding mandatory for UPI app registration

### 3.2 System Controls (Effective August 2025)
- Daily limits on balance enquiry and "list accounts" API calls to prevent abuse
- Regulated processing window for autopay mandate scheduling
- Technical and operational restrictions to enhance system stability and reduce fraud
- System limits on API calls to prevent overload attacks

### 3.3 Data Storage and Localisation
- All payment system data must be stored only in India (RBI data localisation directive, April 2018)
- Payment System Operators must ensure entire data relating to payment systems operated by them is stored in a system only in India
- This includes full end-to-end transaction details, information collected, carried, processed as part of the message or payment instruction
- Foreign leg of transactions may be stored abroad but domestic data must have a copy in India

## CHAPTER 4: RBI DIGITAL LENDING GUIDELINES

### 4.1 Digital Lending Framework (Effective September 2022)
The RBI issued comprehensive guidelines for digital lending covering:
(a) All lending done through digital channels including apps and web platforms
(b) Lending facilitated by Lending Service Providers (LSPs) on behalf of Regulated Entities (REs)
(c) Lending by Default Loss Guarantee (FLDG) arrangements

### 4.2 Key Requirements
- Loan disbursement must be directly into borrower's bank account (not through third-party pool accounts)
- All fees and charges must be disclosed upfront to the borrower
- Cooling-off period: Borrowers have the right to exit digital loans by repaying principal and proportionate APR without penalty
- Key Fact Statement (KFS) must be provided to borrower before loan execution containing:
  * Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
  * Recovery mechanism
  * Grievance redressal details
  * Cooling-off period details

### 4.3 Data Collection Restrictions
- Digital lending apps can only collect data with explicit borrower consent
- Data collection limited to what is necessary for the purpose
- Biometric data collection prohibited
- Access to mobile phone resources (contacts, photos, storage) prohibited unless necessary for the lending service
- One-time access permitted with explicit consent for specific purposes only

### 4.4 FLDG (First Loss Default Guarantee) Guidelines
- Maximum FLDG cap: 5% of the loan portfolio of the respective RE
- FLDG may be in the form of cash deposit, fixed deposit, or bank guarantee
- FLDG arrangements must be in written contracts

## CHAPTER 5: PREPAID PAYMENT INSTRUMENTS (PPI)

### 5.1 Types of PPIs
(a) Small PPIs: Maximum balance Rs. 10,000, can be issued with minimum KYC
(b) Full-KYC PPIs: Maximum balance Rs. 2,00,000
(c) PPIs for Mass Transit Systems: Maximum balance Rs. 3,000

### 5.2 KYC Requirements for PPIs
- Minimum KYC PPIs: OTP-based verification, limited functionality
- Full KYC PPIs: Aadhaar-based e-KYC, video-based KYC, or in-person verification
- Interoperability mandatory for full-KYC PPIs (can be used across merchants and for P2P transfers)

### 5.3 Loading Limits
- Cash loading at PPI agent locations: Maximum Rs. 50,000 per month
- Loading from bank account: As per PPI category limits
- PPIs cannot be loaded from credit lines (unless specifically permitted by RBI)

## CHAPTER 6: KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC) NORMS

### 6.1 Customer Due Diligence
RBI KYC Master Direction requires:
(a) Customer identification and verification using Officially Valid Documents (OVDs):
  - Passport
  - Driving License
  - Voter's Identity Card
  - Aadhaar (issued by UIDAI)
  - NREGA Job Card
  - Letter from National Population Register

(b) Types of KYC:
  - Aadhaar-based e-KYC (using OTP or biometric)
  - Video-based Customer Identification Process (V-CIP)
  - In-person verification
  - Central KYC (CKYC) Registry lookup
  - Digital KYC using Aadhaar with live photo

### 6.2 Periodic KYC Updates
- High-risk customers: KYC update every 2 years
- Medium-risk customers: KYC update every 8 years
- Low-risk customers: KYC update every 10 years
- Non-compliance may result in account restrictions (partial freezing)

### 6.3 Small Accounts (Simplified KYC)
- Opened with self-certified photograph and signature/thumb impression
- Maximum balance: Rs. 50,000
- Maximum annual credits: Rs. 1,00,000
- Maximum withdrawal per month: Rs. 10,000
- Valid for 12 months (extendable to 24 months if full KYC submitted)

## CHAPTER 7: RBI OMBUDSMAN FOR DIGITAL TRANSACTIONS

### 7.1 Coverage
The RBI Ombudsman Scheme covers complaints related to:
- Mobile banking and internet banking
- UPI transactions
- BHIM app transactions
- Prepaid payment instruments (wallets)
- NEFT, RTGS, IMPS transactions
- Card transactions (debit, credit, prepaid)
- Aadhaar-enabled payment systems

### 7.2 Complaint Filing
- Complaints can be filed online at https://cms.rbi.org.in
- No fee charged for filing complaints
- Complaints must first be filed with the service provider
- If unresolved within 30 days, or if unsatisfied with resolution, complaint may be escalated to RBI Ombudsman
- Ombudsman must dispose of complaint within 30 days of receipt

### 7.3 Liability Framework for Unauthorized Transactions
- Zero liability: If caused by bank/system negligence, or third-party breach where customer reports within 3 working days
- Limited liability (max Rs. 10,000): If reported between 4-7 working days
- Full liability: If reported after 7 working days, as determined by bank policy
- Banks must credit "shadow reversal" within 10 working days of complaint, pending investigation
